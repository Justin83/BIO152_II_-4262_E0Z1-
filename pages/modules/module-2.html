Synopsis

This week focuses on how biologists organize living things and use evidence to infer evolutionary relationships. You will practice reading phylogenetic trees, recognizing common ancestry, and connecting classification to the broader history of life. Chapter 25 extends that foundation by placing biological diversity in the context of Earth history and early life. Exam 1 closes the first unit and covers Chapters 22, 23, and 25.
