Synopsis

Chapter 53 examines how and why animals behave as they do, including proximate and ultimate explanations, innate and learned behavior, communication, and the influence of neural, hormonal, developmental, genetic, and evolutionary processes.

Chapter 54 shifts from individuals to populations. You will examine population size and density, dispersion, demography, survivorship, exponential and logistic growth, carrying capacity, population regulation, human age structure, and metapopulations. Begin with the videos, then use the slides and chapter readings to organize and deepen the material.
