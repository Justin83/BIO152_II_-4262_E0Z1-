What to Watch / Skip

  • Required: Arthropod overview
    Arthropod diversity and the big features that make the group successful.
  • Required: Segmentation and tagmata
    How body segments can be grouped and specialized into tagmata, such as head, thorax, and abdomen.
  • Required: Exoskeleton and molting
    Chitinous exoskeleton functions, advantages, constraints, and why arthropods must molt to grow.
  • Required: Jointed appendages
    How appendages can be specialized as antennae, mouthparts, wings, or legs.
  • Skip from approximately 8:10 to 41:20 for the required viewing.
    That middle portion is a long group-by-group survey. Use the optional clips below only if you need additional practice with a particular arthropod group.
  • Optional review: Chelicerates
    Spiders, scorpions, ticks, and relatives; chelicerae, cephalothorax, and major group traits.
  • Optional review: Crustaceans
    Crabs, crayfish, shrimp, and relatives; antennae, appendage specialization, and gill gas exchange.
  • Optional review: Hexapods and insects
    Six-legged arthropods and key insect features.
  • Required: Metamorphosis
    Compare no metamorphosis, incomplete metamorphosis, and complete metamorphosis.
  • Optional review: Myriapods
    Centipede versus millipede body plans, feeding, defense, and leg arrangement.
  • Course terminology note
    The lecturer uses some classification ranks differently from Raven. For the course, use the group names and vocabulary emphasized in Raven, SmartBook, and the PowerPoint.

What to Watch For

This is the main Week 5 Watch This resource. Required sections total about 10½ minutes: 0:00–8:10 and 41:20–43:45.

Be able to connect segmentation, tagmata, jointed appendages, chitinous exoskeletons, molting, and metamorphosis to arthropod success.

As you watch, compare the advantages of an exoskeleton (support, protection, reduced water loss, muscle attachment) with the tradeoff that growth requires molting.
