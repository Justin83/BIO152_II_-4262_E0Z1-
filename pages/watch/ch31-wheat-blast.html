Chapters in This Video

  • Plants, Human Health, and the Scale of the Food Challenge
    11 plant species supply 90% of human calories. The world needs ~70% more food by 2050, and up to 30% of current production is lost to disease and pests before consumption.
  • Wheat: The World's Most Widely Planted Crop
    Wheat is the #2 food crop globally, providing 17% of human calories and planted on more acreage than any other crop.
  • What Is Wheat Blast?
    Caused by Magnaporthe oryzae triticum pathotype; discovered Brazil 1985, spread to Bolivia and Paraguay. Reduces yields 50–90%; a 2009 epidemic cut Brazil's national wheat production 30%. Fungicides ineffective.
  • U.S. Research Response (2008): Four Goals
    USDA-funded team at Kansas State University and Fort Detrick: (1) identify resistant U.S. varieties, (2) develop molecular detection tools, (3) study pathogen biology, (4) educate stakeholders. 500+ wheat varieties tested.
  • Project Two: Forecasting, Fungicides, Disease Cycle
    A second USDA project adds: a blast forecasting system, improved fungicide strategies, and a better understanding of the complete disease cycle.
  • Conclusion: Protecting the Plants We Depend On
    Feeding 9 billion people requires both producing more food and protecting it from disease. Wheat is too important to leave undefended.

What to Watch For

This short video connects Chapter 31 (Fungi) to global food security. Magnaporthe oryzae is a fungal pathogen in the same major group (Ascomycota) as many other plant disease agents. Notice how fungal diseases can be economically catastrophic — a single outbreak reduced an entire nation's wheat production by 30%.

Pay attention to the research design: scientists tested over 500 wheat varieties for resistance vs. susceptibility and are developing molecular detection tools that can identify the pathogen even when symptoms look like other diseases. This is applied mycology with direct consequences for human food supply.
