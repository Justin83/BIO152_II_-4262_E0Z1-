Chapters in This Video

  • Introduction: What Is Fungi?
    Overview of fungi diversity — mushrooms, yeasts, and lichen — plus their symbiotic and decomposer roles.
  • Phylogeny: Closer to Animals Than Plants
    On the eukaryote tree, fungi share more recent common ancestry with animals than with plants.
  • The Five Phyla: A-to-Z Overview
    Introduction to the five monophyletic groups using the A-B-C-G-Z mnemonic.
  • Shared Characteristics of Fungi
    Eukaryotic, heterotrophic, chitin cell walls, and filamentous hyphae — but no true tissues.
  • Heterotrophy: Decomposers & Parasites
    Fungi digesting rotting wood, molding fruit, causing athlete's foot, and trapping nematode worms.
  • Reproduction: Asexual & Sexual Spores
    Asexual spore dispersal from puff balls and sexual reproduction in basidiomycete fruiting bodies.
  • Ascomycota: Sac Fungi
    Cup/sac fungi — morels, yeasts, and penicillin-producing molds — with spores formed inside the ascus.
  • Basidiomycota: Club Fungi
    The familiar cap mushrooms; club-shaped basidia on gills each bear four sexual spores.
  • Chytridiomycota: The Chytrids
    Aquatic, flagellated fungi — once classified as protists — linked to global frog population crashes.
  • Glomerulomycota: Mycorrhizal Fungi
    Form mycorrhizal symbioses with nearly all plant roots, greatly increasing water and nutrient uptake.
  • Zygomycota: Bread Molds
    Named for resistant zygosporangia that burst and spread spores; familiar as the fuzzy mold on bread.

What to Watch For

This Bozeman Science video is your visual guide to Chapter 31: Fungi. Focus on the two key ways fungi feed: as decomposers (breaking down dead organic matter) and as parasites (athlete's foot, nematode traps). Also watch for the structural vocabulary: hyphae, mycelium, and chitin cell walls.

Pay close attention to the five phyla — use the A–B–C–G–Z trick: Ascomycota (sac fungi, penicillin), Basidiomycota (club fungi, mushrooms), Chytridiomycota (chytrids, frog decline), Glomerulomycota (mycorrhizae, plant symbiosis), and Zygomycota (bread molds). These phyla and their defining structures appear in SmartBook and on exams.
