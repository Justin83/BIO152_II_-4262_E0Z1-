Corrections and Clarifications

Epithelial blood supply: Epithelial tissues are avascular. Capillaries are located in the underlying connective tissue, and materials diffuse across the basement membrane.

Intestinal surface area: The projections on individual intestinal epithelial cells are microvilli. Intestinal villi are larger, multicellular projections of the intestinal lining.

Lung terminology: The microscopic air sacs of the lungs are alveoli, not villi.

Pseudostratified epithelium: It appears multilayered because nuclei occur at different heights. All cells contact the basement membrane, although not all reach the apical surface.

Stretching: Transitional epithelium, rather than cuboidal epithelium generally, is specialized to stretch.

Connective tissue: The correct term is extracellular matrix.

Urination: The detrusor smooth muscle of the urinary bladder contracts while the urethral sphincters relax.

Cardiac coupling: Gap junctions within intercalated discs allow ions and electrical current to pass between cardiac muscle cells and coordinate contraction.

Schwann cells: Schwann cells are neuroglial cells, not neurons. They form myelin in the peripheral nervous system.

What to Watch For

Use this video as the structural foundation for Chapter 41. Be able to move from cells to tissues to organs to organ systems and identify the four primary tissue categories.

For epithelial tissue, focus on the two-part naming system: number of layers plus cell shape. Connect thin tissues with diffusion, taller cells with secretion and absorption, and multiple layers with protection.

For connective tissue, organize examples by the nature of the extracellular matrix. Then compare skeletal, smooth, and cardiac muscle and identify the basic structure of a neuron and the supporting role of neuroglia.

Part 2 will continue Chapter 41 with organ-system organization, homeostasis, negative feedback, antagonistic effectors, Q10, and thermoregulation.
