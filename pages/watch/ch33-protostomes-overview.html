Chapters in This Video

  • Bilaterians, symmetry, and cephalization
    Places protostomes within the bilaterian animals and connects bilateral symmetry to a distinct anterior end with concentrated sensory and nervous tissue.
  • Triploblastic development and mesoderm
    Reviews ectoderm, endoderm, mesoderm, and why the mesoderm supports more complex tissues and organ systems.
  • Protostomes versus deuterostomes
    Uses the classic blastopore comparison as a brief connection back to Chapter 32.
  • Shared protostome organization
    Introduces the anterior brain around the digestive-tract opening and the ventral nervous-system pattern found across many protostomes.
  • Two major protostome lineages
    Compares lophotrochozoans with ecdysozoans, especially the role of molting in the ecdysozoan lineage.
  • Lophophores and trochophore larvae
    Explains the structures that give lophotrochozoans their name and connects them to feeding, gas exchange, and larval development.
  • Annelid segmentation
    Shows how repeated segments can improve movement control and permit regional specialization.
  • Cuticles and molting
    Explains why a cuticle provides protection and support but must be shed and replaced for growth.
  • Arthropod exoskeletons and appendages
    Introduces chitin, exoskeletons, segmentation, and specialized jointed appendages — ideas expanded in the next Watch This page.

What to Watch For

This is the main Chapter 33 overview. Use it to organize the individual groups in the reading rather than trying to memorize an isolated list of animal phyla.

Be able to distinguish the two broad protostome lineages: Lophotrochozoa and Ecdysozoa. Associate lophophores, trochophore larvae, and annelid segmentation with the former; associate a cuticle and molting with the latter.

The video uses the classic blastopore comparison as an introduction. As in Chapter 32, treat that as a useful broad developmental pattern rather than an absolute rule. For course terminology, use Ecdysozoa / ecdysozoans in your reading, SmartBook, and assessments.

The final section intentionally leads into arthropods. Complete the next Watch This page for focused practice with exoskeletons, molting, tagmata, jointed appendages, and metamorphosis.
